Working in distributed teams

Seems these days that working remotely is the norm. Actually, it's kinda rare to find a dev whose criteria for the perfect job doesn't include remote -or at least hybrid- at the very top. Well, we're lucky that so many opportunities exist. However, this means that whether you're part of a multinational corporation or a small, remote startup; understanding best practices for effective collaboration can significantly impact your team's productivity and success.



Communication is Key

Effective communication is the cornerstone of any successful team, especially when members are geographically dispersed. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

• Choose the right communication tool: While email is a staple, if it's up to you, consider using tools like Slack for real time messaging, or Zoom for real-time discussions and video conferencing.
• Set clear expectations: Ensure everyone understands their roles and that everyone understand yours. Always make sure that deadlines are well synced across the entire team, project management tools are great to keep track of tasks and progress.
• Find the best communication threshold: While it's usually better share too much information than too little. You still need to not over do it. As the cons of little communications are obvious; overly redundant, repetitive communication can lead to fatigue and confusion. Always thrive to have a single source of truth which everyone can check out at the same time. I can't count the number of times I received the same info or numbers in multiple different sheets.
• Be mindful of time zones: Schedule meetings and calls at times that work for everyone. Luckily, most calendar apps now make this task easy. However, keep in mind that not all team members will have the same energy levels at the same time. For instance, make sure not to schedule a meeting where one team member is at the start of his day while another is at their very last time slot.

Build trust and relationships

Building trust and strong relationships is essential for effective collaboration in distributed teams. Here's how you can foster these connections:

• Regular check-ins: Schedule regular one-on-one meetings with your team members to discuss their progress, challenges, and goals.
• Encourage social interactions: Organize virtual team-building activities or casual chats to help team members get to know each other better.
• Be transparent and honest: Open communication is crucial for building trust. Be honest about your challenges and successes.

Leverage technology

Technology can be a powerful tool for bridging the physical distance between team members. Here are some technologies to consider:

• Video conferencing: Use tools like Zoom or Webex for face-to-face meetings.
• Project management tools: Tools like Jira or Asana can help you organize tasks, track progress, and improve collaboration.
• Time tracking software: Time tracking tools can help you stay accountable and ensure that everyone is contributing their fair share.
• Cloud-based collaboration tools: Tools like Google Drive or Dropbox allow you to collaborate on documents and files in real time. However, be very careful not to use personal accounts if you're working with more than a team of four. Otherwise, keeping track of file ownership and share permissions would require its own dedicated time slot. Usually, business editions of these software products allow for much easier permission controls and the ability to reclaim ownership of files in the event of a team member's departure.

Cultural Sensitivity

When working with team members from different cultures, it's important to be mindful of cultural differences. Here are some tips:

• Learn about different cultures: Take the time to learn about the customs, values, and communication styles of your team members.
• Be respectful: Avoid making assumptions or stereotypes about people from different cultures.
• Adapt your communication style: Be flexible and willing to adjust your communication style to accommodate different cultural preferences.



Code collaboration and review

Effective code collaboration and review are essential for maintaining high-quality code in distributed teams. Here are some best practices:

• Version control: Use a version control system like Git to track changes to your code and collaborate effectively. No zip files, or final final folders.
• Code reviews: Conduct regular code reviews to identify potential issues and improve code quality. Make sure to have both your code reviewed as well as the you reviewing other's code as well.
• Coding standards: Establish and follow coding standards to ensure consistency and maintainability. Use lint tools and plugins to assist with this.
• Pair programming: Consider pairing programmers to share knowledge, improve code quality, and reduce errors. Make sure your get equal parts of being the observer and driver.

Remember, the key to success in distributed teams is to embrace the challenges and find creative solutions. With a little effort and a positive attitude, you can overcome any obstacles and achieve your team's goals. While it can be challenging, with the right strategies and tools, it can also be highly rewarding.
